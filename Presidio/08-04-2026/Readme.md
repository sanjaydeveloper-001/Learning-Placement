### ✍️ Quantitative Aptitude (3 MCQs)
This section tests your mathematical and analytical skills. Focus on speed and accuracy.
1.  **Percentages**: If the price of a product is increased by 25%, by what percentage must it be decreased to bring it back to the original price?
    *   a) 20%
    *   b) 25%
    *   c) 30%
    *   d) 35%
    > **Answer:** a) 20% **Explanation:** Let the original price be 100. After a 25% increase, it becomes 125. To get back to 100, we need a decrease of 25 from 125, which is a (25/125) * 100 = 20% decrease.
2.  **Time and Work**: A can complete a task in 10 days, and B can complete the same task in 15 days. In how many days will they complete the task if they work together?
    *   a) 5 days
    *   b) 6 days
    *   c) 7 days
    *   d) 8 days
    > **Answer:** b) 6 days **Explanation:** A's 1-day work = 1/10, B's 1-day work = 1/15. Their combined 1-day work = (1/10 + 1/15) = 5/30 = 1/6. Therefore, they will finish in 6 days.
3.  **Ratios**: The ratio of two numbers is 3:5. If their sum is 80, what is the larger number?
    *   a) 30
    *   b) 40
    *   c) 50
    *   d) 60
    > **Answer:** c) 50 **Explanation:** Let the numbers be 3x and 5x. Their sum is 8x = 80, so x = 10. The larger number is 5x = 5*10 = 50.
4.  **Averages**: The average of five numbers is 18. If one number is excluded, the average becomes 16. Find the excluded number.
    *   a) 24
    *   b) 25
    *   c) 26
    *   d) 27
    > **Answer:** c) 26 **Explanation:** Sum of five numbers = 18 * 5 = 90. Sum of four numbers = 16 * 4 = 64. Excluded number = 90 - 64 = 26.
5.  **Profit & Loss**: An article is sold for ₹720 at a profit of 20%. What is its cost price?
    *   a) ₹500
    *   b) ₹550
    *   c) ₹600
    *   d) ₹650
    > **Answer:** c) ₹600 **Explanation:** If SP is 120% of CP (100% + 20%), then CP = (SP / 120) * 100 = (720 / 120) * 100 = 6 * 100 = ₹600.
6.  **Simple Interest**: What is the simple interest on ₹2000 at a rate of 5% per annum for 3 years?
    *   a) ₹200
    *   b) ₹250
    *   c) ₹300
    *   d) ₹350
    > **Answer:** c) ₹300 **Explanation:** SI = (P * R * T)/100 = (2000 * 5 * 3)/100 = 20 * 5 * 3 = ₹300.
7.  **Number System**: What is the sum of all prime numbers between 10 and 20?
    *   a) 60
    *   b) 61
    *   c) 70
    *   d) 71
    > **Answer:** a) 60 **Explanation:** The prime numbers between 10 and 20 are 11, 13, 17, and 19. Their sum is 11 + 13 + 17 + 19 = 60.
8.  **Speed, Distance, Time**: If a train travels 300 km in 5 hours, what is its speed in m/s?
    *   a) 50/3 m/s
    *   b) 60/5 m/s
    *   c) 100/6 m/s
    *   d) 100/3 m/s
    > **Answer:** a) 50/3 m/s **Explanation:** Speed = 300 km / 5 hrs = 60 km/hr. To convert to m/s, multiply by 5/18: 60 * (5/18) = (60/18)*5 = (10/3)*5 = 50/3 m/s.
9.  **Permutation & Combination**: In how many ways can a committee of 3 people be chosen from a group of 7 people?
    *   a) 21
    *   b) 35
    *   c) 42
    *   d) 210
    > **Answer:** b) 35 **Explanation:** The number of ways is 7C3 = 7! / (3! * 4!) = (7*6*5) / (3*2*1) = 35.
10. **Probability**: What is the probability of drawing an ace from a standard deck of 52 playing cards?
    *   a) 1/13
    *   b) 1/26
    *   c) 1/52
    *   d) 1/4
    > **Answer:** a) 1/13 **Explanation:** There are 4 aces in a deck of 52 cards. Probability = 4/52 = 1/13.

### 🧩 Logical Reasoning (3 MCQs)

1.  **Number Series**: Find the next term in the series: 2, 5, 10, 17, 26, ?
    *   a) 35
    *   b) 36

    *   a) 0 km
    *   b) 1 km
    *   c) 2 km
    *   d) 3 km

    *   b) Leaves
    *   c) Roots
    *   d) Branch
    > **Answer:** b) Leaves **Explanation:** A book is made up of pages; a tree is made up of leaves.
5.  **Coding-Decoding**: If 'TABLE' is coded as 'GZYOV', how is 'CHAIR' coded?
    *   a) XSZRI
    *   b) XSZIR
    *   c) XZSRI
    *   d) XSZRI
    > **Answer:** a) XSZRI **Explanation:** Each letter is replaced by the letter at the opposite end of the alphabet (A=1, Z=26). T(20) -> G(7), A(1)->Z(26), B(2)->Y(25), L(12)->O(15), E(5)->V(22). So CHAIR: C(3)->X(24), H(8)->S(19), A(1)->Z(26), I(9)->R(18), R(18)->I(9).
6.  **Syllogism**: Statements: All pens are pencils. No pencil is sharpener. Conclusions: I. No pen is sharpener. II. Some pencils are pens. Which conclusion(s) follow(s)?
    *   a) Only I follows
    *   b) Only II follows
    *   c) Both I and II follow
    *   d) Neither I nor II follows
    > **Answer:** c) Both I and II follow **Explanation:** Since all pens are pencils, and no pencil is a sharpener, no pen can be a sharpener. Also, the statement "All pens are pencils" implies that some pencils are definitely pens.
7.  **Logical Puzzles**: If 3 cats can catch 3 mice in 3 minutes, how many cats are needed to catch 100 mice in 100 minutes?
    *   a) 3
    *   b) 10
    *   c) 100
    *   d) 300
    > **Answer:** a) 3 **Explanation:** 3 cats catch 3 mice in 3 minutes means 3 cats catch 1 mouse per minute. So 3 cats can catch 100 mice in 100 minutes.
8.  **Odd One Out**: Find the odd one out: (a) Apple (b) Mango (c) Orange (d) Banana
    *   a) Apple
    *   b) Mango
    *   c) Orange
    *   d) Banana
    > **Answer:** c) Orange **Explanation:** All others are typically eaten with their skin, while oranges are peeled.
9.  **Clock & Calendar**: If 1st January 2020 was a Wednesday, what day of the week was 1st January 2021?
    *   a) Thursday
    *   b) Friday
    *   c) Saturday
    *   d) Sunday
    > **Answer:** b) Friday **Explanation:** 2020 was a leap year, so it had 366 days, which is 52 weeks and 2 odd days. So the day shifts by 2 days. Wednesday + 2 = Friday.
10. **Data Sufficiency**: Find if `x + y` is even. Statement I: `x` is even. Statement II: `y` is odd.
    *   a) Statement I alone is sufficient
    *   b) Statement II alone is sufficient
    *   c) Both statements together are sufficient
    *   d) Neither statement is sufficient
    > **Answer:** d) Neither statement is sufficient **Explanation:** To determine if `x + y` is even, we need to know the parity (evenness/oddness) of both numbers. Neither statement alone gives both, and together they tell us one is even and one is odd, so the sum is odd (not even).

1.  **What will be the output of the following C code?**
    ```c
    #include <stdio.h>
    int main() {
        int a = 5;
        printf("%d ", a++);
        printf("%d", ++a);
        return 0;
    }
    ```
    *   a) 5 6
    *   b) 5 7
    *   c) 6 6
    *   d) 6 7
    > **Answer:** b) 5 7 **Explanation:** `a++` is post-increment, so it prints the current value (5) and then increments `a` to 6. Then `++a` is pre-increment, so it increments `a` from 6 to 7 and then prints the new value.

### ✍️ Subjective SQL Queries (2 Questions)
1.  **Problem:** Write a query to get the second-highest salary from an `employees` table (columns: `id`, `name`, `salary`).
    *   **Solution:**
        ```sql
        -- Method 1: Using LIMIT and OFFSET (Simplest)
        SELECT salary FROM employees ORDER BY salary DESC LIMIT 1 OFFSET 1;

        -- Method 2: Using a Subquery (More portable)
        SELECT MAX(salary) FROM employees WHERE salary < (SELECT MAX(salary) FROM employees);
        ```
    > **Explanation:** The first method sorts salaries descending and skips the first (highest) to get the second. The second method finds the maximum salary that is less than the absolute maximum.

2.  **Problem:** Find all duplicate `email` addresses in a `users` table (columns: `id`, `name`, `email`).
    *   **Solution:**
        ```sql
        SELECT email, COUNT(*)
        FROM users
        GROUP BY email
        HAVING COUNT(*) > 1;
        ```
    > **Explanation:** `GROUP BY email` groups all rows with the same email. `HAVING COUNT(*) > 1` filters to show only those groups with more than one occurrence, which indicates duplicates.

### 💻 Subjective Programming Questions (3 Questions)
1.  **Problem:** Write pseudocode to check if a given number is prime.
    *   **Solution (Pseudocode):**
        ```plaintext
        FUNCTION isPrime(number)
            IF number <= 1 THEN
                RETURN False
            END IF
            FOR i FROM 2 TO SQRT(number) DO
                IF number MOD i == 0 THEN
                    RETURN False
                END IF
            END FOR
            RETURN True
        END FUNCTION
        ```
    > **Explanation:** A prime number is greater than 1 and has no positive divisors other than 1 and itself. The loop checks for any divisor up to the square root of the number for efficiency.

2.  **Problem:** Write code (in C, C++, or Java) to find the sum of all elements in an integer array.
    *   **Solution (C):**
        ```c
        #include <stdio.h>

        int main() {
            int arr[] = {10, 20, 30, 40, 50};
            int n = sizeof(arr) / sizeof(arr[0]);
            int sum = 0;

            for (int i = 0; i < n; i++) {
                sum += arr[i];
            }

            printf("Sum of array elements is: %d\n", sum);
            return 0;
        }
        ```
    > **Explanation:** This code calculates the size of the array, then iterates through it, adding each element to a `sum` variable. This is a fundamental and commonly asked array manipulation problem.

3.  **Problem:** Write pseudocode to reverse a given string.
    *   **Solution (Pseudocode):**
        ```plaintext
        FUNCTION reverseString(inputString)
            SET reversedString = ""
            FOR i FROM LENGTH(inputString) - 1 DOWNTO 0 DO
                reversedString = reversedString + inputString[i]
            END FOR
            RETURN reversedString
        END FUNCTION
        ```
    > **Explanation:** The pseudocode initializes an empty `reversedString`. It then iterates through the `inputString` backwards, appending each character to build the reversed version.
